Nightlife Areas Around Asok

Step off at BTS Asok station and you'll find Bangkok's most iconic nightlife spots all within a 10-minute walk. If you want to make the most of Bangkok after dark, getting the lay of this area first will make everything easier.

🐄
Must-See Soi Cowboy
Sukhumvit Soi 23 / 3-min walk from BTS Asok

A roughly 150-meter alley connecting Sukhumvit Soi 21 and Soi 23, lined with around 40 go-go bars on both sides. This is Bangkok's most famous entertainment strip. It traces its origins to the 1970s when an American veteran opened a bar here, and today it draws a cosmopolitan mix of Western and Asian tourists. The neon lights and pulsing music make it a quintessential Bangkok experience worth seeing at least once.

🕗 8:00 PM – 2:00 AM
💰 Drinks from 150–200 THB
👥 Solo & up
🍺
Laid-back Beer Bars around Soi 23
Sukhumvit Soi 23 / 5-min walk from BTS Asok

Less flashy than go-go bars, beer bars let you chat casually with the girls over a counter drink. Prices are lower too, making them a popular second stop after the sensory overload of a go-go bar. The relaxed vibe is welcoming even for first-time visitors to Bangkok.

🕗 7:00 PM – 1:00 AM
💰 Drinks from 100–150 THB
👥 Best for 1–2 people
🥂
Great for Groups Private Karaoke Rooms near Soi 23
Sukhumvit Soi 23 / 7-min walk from BTS Asok

A fully private room where you can invite girls to join your party and enjoy drinks together. The biggest difference from go-go bars? Your entire group stays in the same room. At go-go bars, everyone tends to split up, but in a private room, 2 to 6 people can share the exact same experience. As the reviews below show, group travelers overwhelmingly rate this format higher in satisfaction.

🕗 8:00 PM – 1:00 AM
💰 From 2,500 THB / person
👥 Best for groups of 2–6
💡 The Classic Night Out Route: Start at Soi Cowboy to soak in the atmosphere (about 1 hour as sightseeing) → grab a drink at a beer bar → then head to a private karaoke room where the whole group can have a blast. This is the go-to itinerary for the area — and everything is within walking distance, so no taxi needed.

Soi Cowboy: Key Venue Guide

There are around 40 bars on Soi Cowboy, but here are the three that are especially popular with international travelers.

🏆 Baccara

The most popular long-standing venue among Japanese visitors. Many of the girls speak some Japanese, making it a comfortable choice for first-timers. It's a large venue with plenty of selection. Drinks run around 150–180 THB. The interior was renovated in 2025 with a cyberpunk-inspired redesign that's worth seeing in itself.

🦈 Shark

Right next to Baccara and equally popular. Known for a younger roster of girls and an upbeat atmosphere. They've introduced a smart bar system where you order and settle your tab via tablet, which virtually eliminates billing surprises — a nice peace of mind.

🏠 Crazy House

Known for more daring outfits compared to other venues. Best for those seeking a wilder experience. Prices tend to be slightly higher.

⚠️ Common Go-Go Bar Mistakes: Buying round after round of lady drinks can make your bill skyrocket fast. If you're considering a bar fine (taking a girl out), always confirm the terms upfront before ordering — that's the golden rule.

Which one should you pick? It depends on your trip's purpose and group size. Here's an honest side-by-side comparison.

When groups go to go-go bars, we hear the same story over and over: "Everyone wandered off to find their own girl, and it turned into separate solo experiences." If you want the whole crew to relive "that night" together long after you're home, a private room is hands-down the better choice.

What Group Travelers Need to Know

📊 Budget Breakdown (Go-Go Bars)

Your own drinks run 150–180 THB, lady drinks 170–200 THB, and a bar fine 600–700 THB. Over a couple of hours with 3–4 drinks for yourself and 2–3 for the girls, you're typically looking at 2,000–3,000 THB total.

⏰ Best Time to Go

Soi Cowboy starts getting busy around 8 PM. Aim to arrive before happy hour ends at 9 PM — that's when you get the widest selection of girls at the lowest prices. After 10 PM, the crowds thicken and the most popular girls are already spoken for.

ℹ️ Honest Take from the Writer: Go-go bars are worth visiting once as a classic Bangkok experience. But they're not great for groups looking to spend a long evening together. After about an hour of soaking in the atmosphere, moving to a private karaoke room is the best way to maximize both value and everyone's enjoyment.

🍽 Before You Head Out: Recommended Dinner Spots

Fuel up before your night out. The snack bars around Asok don't really get going until after 10 PM, so the golden pattern is to enjoy a leisurely dinner around 8 PM nearby and then head over.

🏠
Thai Cuisine THE LOCAL
32/1 Soi Sukhumvit 23 / Approx. 10-min walk from BTS Asok

An authentic Thai restaurant housed in a renovated traditional home halfway down Soi 23. Staff in traditional Thai attire welcome you into a stunning space with real atmosphere — upscale enough for group dinners and business entertaining.

The top recommendation is the beef tongue massaman curry. The thick-cut tongue melts in your mouth, and once you've tried it, you'll be planning your next visit. The khao soi is equally impressive — rich and deeply flavored, leagues above what you'll find at a typical street-side eatery. The starters, including the pomelo salad, are all high quality, making it the perfect spot to eat well before heading out for the night.

The annex building has private rooms on the second floor (additional room charge applies), accommodating larger parties, farewell dinners, or business entertaining. Working back from the 10 PM last order, arriving at 8 PM is ideal timing. Head to a snack bar at 9:30 PM, then drift over to Soi Cowboy later in the night — that's our recommended course.

🕗 11:00 AM – 10:00 PM
📅 Open daily
💰 Approx. 2,000–3,000 THB / person
👥 Groups & private rooms available
📞 02-664-0664
Official Website → Google Maps

New to Bangkok? A Quick Guide for Expats

Just relocated to Bangkok for work? The Asok / Sukhumvit 23 area is one of the most expat-friendly nightlife zones in the city. Here's what you need to know to feel comfortable from your very first night out.

🏙 Why Expats Love This Area: Sukhumvit Soi 23 is a 10-minute walk from BTS Asok — easy to get to and easy to get home from. Unlike Nana or Patpong, the vibe here is relatively low-key, and many venues have English-speaking staff. It's the go-to area for corporate entertaining, team nights out, and casual after-work drinks.
💼
Expat Tip Corporate Entertaining & Receipt-Ready Venues
Sukhumvit Soi 23 area

If you're entertaining clients or colleagues, Club LINE23 is one of the few venues in Bangkok that issues Tax ID receipts — useful for expense reporting. The fully private room format is also ideal for professional entertaining: no awkward run-ins with other customers, and the atmosphere is relaxed enough for business conversation.

For dinner before heading out, The Local is a reliable choice for business entertaining — upscale enough to impress, with private room options on the second floor. Staff are accustomed to hosting corporate groups.

🗺
First Timer Bangkok Nightlife 101: What to Expect
  • Go-Go bars (like Soi Cowboy) are tourist attractions — worth seeing once, but not the main event for most expats.
  • Snack bars & private karaoke are more social, great for groups, and easier to manage your spending.
  • Always check if a venue has English-speaking staff before going — it makes a big difference on your first visit.
  • Grab an Uber or Bolt app — the area is well-connected, but late-night taxis can overcharge tourists.
